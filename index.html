About Me

I am a Research Engineer at Styldod Inc. where I develop AI-powered solutions that transform how people visualize and interact with real-estate imagery. My day-to-day work spans the full lifecycle of project development - starting with ideation, assessing user experience requirements, and evaluating technical feasibility. I plan project workflows, conduct extensive literature reviews of existing methods, and experiment with SoTA methods for our use case. When required, I fine-tune methods on our data and requirements (e.g. image editing models for image enhancement). For challenges without existing solutions—such as instance segmentation of walls—or where existing methods fall short—like 3D bounding box detection from surface-only point clouds derived from monocular depth—I develop novel methodologies. I also build prototypes, iterate through revisions based on qualitative evaluation, and deliver final production-ready builds.

I have completed my Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering with a Specialization in Visual Computing from Indian Institute of Technology, Jodhpur. During my time there, I worked with Prof. Rajendra Nagar on 3D surface representation using compactly supported Radial Basis Functions, exploring memory efficient learnable parametric representation. Later, I worked with Prof. Avinash Sharma at 3DVisLab on Face Reenactment using diffusion models conditioned on 3D Face Morphable Models. My research experiences at IIT Jodhpur deeply shaped my interest in visual computing and inspired me to pursue a career in research.

Research Interests

My research interest lies in the intersection of 3D Computer Vision and Applied Research. I am particularly interested in 3D Humans, 3D Reconstruction, 3D Scene Understanding and Reasoning.
My industry experience has provided me with valuable insights into how AI and 3D vision are transforming real estate and interior design. This has sparked my interest in applying my research to solve real-world problems in this domain.
